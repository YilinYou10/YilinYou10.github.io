About

My work lies at the intersection of building physics, advanced glazing materials, and urban building energy modelling. I am particularly interested in the development and evaluation of thermotropic and thermochromic smart window systems that can dynamically regulate solar heat gain and daylight availability in buildings.

Through experimental measurements, EnergyPlus simulation, and city-scale energy assessment, my research aims to support the design of climate-adaptive building envelopes and low-carbon urban environments.

Research Interests

Smart Windows

Thermotropic and thermochromic glazing, hydrogel-based smart windows, solar modulation, and adaptive façade systems.

Building Energy Simulation

EnergyPlus modelling, building envelope optimization, cooling and heating energy performance, and simulation-based design.

Daylighting and Comfort

Daylight availability, useful daylight illuminance, glare control, indoor thermal environment, and occupant comfort.

Urban Energy Modelling

City-scale building energy assessment, future climate scenarios, retrofit potential, and low-carbon urban development.

Selected Projects

All-Climate Thermotropic Smart Window
Development and performance assessment of a seasonal adaptive thermotropic smart window system for improving building energy efficiency and daylighting comfort.
City-Scale Assessment of Thermotropic Glazing
GIS-based urban building energy modelling to evaluate the impact of thermotropic glazing under present and future climate scenarios.
Hydrogel-Based Thermotropic Glazing
Experimental and numerical investigation of hydrogel-based thermotropic glazing systems for solar heat gain control and daylight regulation.
